               
Case Study :
Phishing is a common cybercrime where attackers try to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trustworthy source. It usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites.

In a typical phishing attack, a user receives a message that appears to be from a bank or a trusted organization. The message often creates urgency, asking the user to click on a link and update their details. When the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks real. Once they enter their information, it is captured by the attacker.

Students and online users are common targets because they may not always verify the authenticity of such messages. The consequences of phishing can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

This type of cybercrime highlights the importance of being cautious while using the internet and verifying sources before sharing any sensitive information.
Prevention Checklist
Use strong and unique passwords
Do not click on unknown links
Verify website URLs before entering data
Enable two-factor authentication
Never share OTPs with anyone
Regularly update passwords
Avoid using public Wi-Fi for transactions
Monitor bank account activity regularly
